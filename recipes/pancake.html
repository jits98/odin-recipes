Pancake

Description

A pancake is a type of flat cake that is typically thin and round, made from a batter of starch, eggs, milk, and butter or oil. It is cooked on a hot surface, such as a griddle or frying pan, and can be served as a sweet or savory dish. Pancakes can be found in various forms and flavors around the world, with different cultures adding their own twists to the recipe. In terms of texture and appearance, pancakes can range from delicate and lacy to dense and cake-like. They can be topped with a variety of ingredients, such as fruit, whipped cream, chocolate chips, or nuts, and can be served as a breakfast dish, snack, or dessert. Some popular types of pancakes include buttermilk pancakes, blueberry pancakes, and banana pancakes, each with its own unique flavor and texture. Overall, pancakes are a versatile and beloved food that can be enjoyed in many different ways, making them a staple in many cuisines around the world.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (1 1/2 to 2 cups)
  • Sugar (1-2 tablespoons)
  • Baking powder (2-4 teaspoons)
  • Salt (1/4 to 1 teaspoon)
  • Milk (1 1/2 to 2 cups)
  • Unsalted butter, melted (2-4 tablespoons)
  • Eggs (2 large)
  • Vegetable oil (for greasing the griddle or pan)

    • Fresh or dried fruits (for added flavor and nutrition)
    • Nuts or seeds (for added texture and flavor)

    • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Steps

  1. Combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of sugar, 1 tablespoon of baking powder, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt in a large bowl.
  2. Whisk until well combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine 1 egg, 1 1/4 cups of milk, 4 tablespoons of melted butter, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract.
  4. Whisk until smooth.
  5. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and whisk until just combined.
  6. Do not overmix.
  7. Allow the batter to rest for 30 minutes to allow the flour to absorb the liquid and the baking powder to activate.
  8. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat.
  9. Grease the pan with a small amount of butter or cooking spray.
  10. Using a 1/4 cup measuring cup, scoop the batter onto the pan.
  11. Cook for 2 minutes or until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges start to dry.
  12. Flip the pancake and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes or until golden brown.
  13. Serve the pancakes hot with your choice of toppings, such as maple syrup, butter, fresh fruit, whipped cream, or powdered sugar.
